## 2. Who's reading

The audience is a small seller or founder who:
- Sells on Etsy / eBay / Amazon / TikTok Shop and feels the squeeze — fees, no control, no brand, no customer data.
- Is launching a product and doesn't have a store yet.
- Is not technical. Doesn't think in "websites." Thinks in *sales, customers, inventory, getting paid.*
- Is busy and skeptical of agencies and of anything that sounds like a pitch.

**Write for them, not for developers.** If a bakery owner or a candle seller wouldn't understand a sentence, rewrite it.

---

## 3. The voice

- **Warm, real, confident.** A knowledgeable friend who happens to build stores — never a salesperson.
- **Short sentences. White space. One idea per post.**
- Plain language. No jargon, no buzzwords, no filler.
- Conversational but never sloppy. Reads like a person, not a brand.
- Leads with the *seller's problem*, not the solution.
- Expertise is **felt, not announced.** The reader should think *"this person gets it — what do they do?"* and never *"this is an ad."*

**Quality bar / benchmarks:** GaryVee (raw, direct), Reid Hoffman (insight-led), Marc Lore (confident simplicity). Match their stopping power.

**Voice in a sentence:** *Plain-spoken, confident, and a little blunt — leads with the seller's pain, respects their intelligence, and lets the expertise show instead of selling it.*

---

## 4. Hard rules (never break)

- **Never sound like an ad.**
- **Never open with the word "I"** — the algorithm suppresses it.
- **Never open with a question as the very first word** — overused, weak.
- **Never say "hire me," "I build websites," or any direct pitch** mid-post. Position indirectly.
- **Banned phrases:** "game-changer," "transform your business," "in today's digital world," "leverage," "synergy," "unlock," "elevate," "passionate about," "solutions provider."
- **No em-dash-stuffed corporate run-ons.** If a sentence doesn't earn its place, cut it.
- **Hashtags only at the bottom**, never mid-post.
- **Length:** 100–200 words. Hard ceiling 250. White space beats word count.

---

## 5. Content pillars (what to post about)

Rotate across these. Don't hammer one.

1. **The marketplace trap** — why Etsy/Amazon/TikTok Shop quietly cost sellers more than they realize (fees, no customer data, no brand, platform risk).
2. **Owning your store** — what changes when you own the storefront instead of renting space on someone else's.
3. **Behind the build** — small, concrete observations from actually building stores (without being technical).
4. **Seller psychology** — what makes people trust a store enough to check out. Conversion, trust, first impressions.
5. **Myth-busting** — "you need a huge budget," "you need to be techy," "a marketplace listing is enough," etc.
6. **Founder mindset** — the reality of launching a product / brand. Empathy posts that make the reader feel seen.

## 6. Post types

Pick the best fit for the topic. Rotate variety over time.

| Type | When to use |
|---|---|
| **Insight** | A surprising truth about selling online / owning a store |
| **Problem** | Surface a pain the seller didn't know they could name |
| **Story** | A small personal moment or observation with a lesson |
| **Myth-bust** | Challenge a common belief sellers hold |
| **List** | Tight, scannable value — 3 to 5 items, no more |
| **Hook-only** | Ultra-short curiosity-gap post built to pull comments |

---

## 7. Post anatomy

**Hook (line 1):** must stop the scroll. Would someone pause mid-feed for this? Use one of:
- A bold, specific claim
- A question that stings a little (just not as the literal first word)
- A surprising stat or observation
- An unexpected comparison

**Body:** one idea, developed simply. Short paragraphs, 1–2 sentences max. White space between every thought. No jargon.

**Positioning:** indirect, always. Let the post make them think *"this person gets it."* Never announce the service.

**CTA — rotate on a 3-post cycle:**
- Post 1: soft CTA — *"Comment if this sounds familiar."*
- Post 2: no CTA — let the value stand alone.
- Post 3: direct but warm — *"If you're still renting space on someone else's platform, let's talk. DM me."*
- When unsure: default to no CTA or soft CTA.

## 10. Formatting

- Single-sentence or two-sentence paragraphs.
- A blank line between nearly every thought — LinkedIn rewards white space.
- No emojis in the body. One is fine in a CTA line at most; default to none.
- Lists: max 3–5 items, short.
- Never bury the hook. Line 1 carries the post.

---

## 11. Posting cadence & timing

- **Frequency:** 3–10x/week. Consistency over volume.
- **Best windows (Central Time):** Tue–Thu, 7–9 AM or 12–1 PM.
- **Avoid for lead-gen:** Friday afternoon, Saturday, Sunday.
- Engagement posts (polls, open questions) can run any weekday.
- **Comment replies:** match the post's tone, ask one follow-up to keep the thread alive, stay 1–3 sentences, never defensive or salesy.

Most sellers think the goal is more traffic.

It's not.

You can send a thousand people to a store that loses them at checkout, and you've spent money to watch them leave.
