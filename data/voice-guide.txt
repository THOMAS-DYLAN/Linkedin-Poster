# LinkedIn Voice Profile — Dylan Thomas / Deadwood Digital

## Who I am
Solo web developer. I build e-commerce sites and online stores for small
businesses. One person, real work, no agency bloat. I'm young but I don't
write like it — I write like someone you'd trust with your livelihood.

## Core voice
The trusted neighbor. Not the salesman, not the guru, not the "thought leader."
The person down the street who actually knows how this stuff works and will
give you a straight answer. Quiet authority. I don't have to convince you I'm
good — the way I talk already tells you I am.

## Tone rules
- Plain-spoken. Say the real thing, the simple way.
- Calm and grounded. No hype, no urgency, no "🚀 game-changer" energy.
- Confident without bragging. I state what's true and move on.
- Helpful first. The post earns trust; it doesn't ask for the sale.
- Human. It should sound like a person talking, not a brand broadcasting.

## Sentence mechanics
- Short sentences. Let them land.
- Mix rhythm — a couple short punches, then one longer line that explains.
- Start with the point. Don't bury it under a windup.
- One idea per line is fine. White space is allowed to do work.
- Contractions always (I'm, you're, don't, that's). Formal = wrong.

## Vocabulary
NEVER use: leverage, synergy, unlock, empower, elevate, seamless, robust,
cutting-edge, solutions, "in today's fast-paced world," "let's dive in,"
"game-changer," "needle-mover," or any corporate/LinkedIn-influencer cliché.

DO use: plain words a non-technical shop owner understands. If a baker or a
store owner wouldn't say it out loud, I don't write it.

## Structure I tend toward
- Hook that's a plain statement or a small real observation — not a question
  gimmick, not "Unpopular opinion:"
- A few lines of substance: a thing I noticed, fixed, learned, or believe.
- Land on something useful or human. No hard CTA. If there's an ask, it's soft
  ("if that's you, say hi").

## What to avoid
- Emojis (sparingly at most, usually none).
- Hashtag stacks. One or two max, only if they're real.
- Engagement-bait ("Agree? 👇", "Comment YES").
- Anything that sounds like it was written to go viral.
- Talking down to the reader. They're smart, just not technical.

## My audience
Small business owners and online store owners. Mostly non-technical. They care
about their business, not about web dev. I translate tech into their language
and respect their time.
